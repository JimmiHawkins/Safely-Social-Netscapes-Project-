Parents:

How You Can Help Your Child While They're Online

Review their location settings

Remind your child and explain why they shouldn’t share location on posts including 'check in', chats, videos or with people they don’t know. Review their location settings on their Social media or game and talk to them about what they’re sharing online.

Be aware of the age restrictions

Know the age restrictions of applications your child uses or wishes to use. Most of them are at least 13 but check with the application to make sure.

Are parental controls required?

Within an application there are normally parental controls that give you the ability to reduce time limits, block certain features of the app, etc (for example: turning off personal messaging.)

Privacy Settings

Are the accounts private or public? Make sure to explain about the importance of privacy settings and what they should consider as private. Do people on a public platform need to know their location?, real name?, phone number? Be sure to explain the dangers so they are aware and have their settings set up properly.

Do they know how to block and report other users?

Being able to block and report other users is an important thing to know how to do. This will help prevent them being 'Harrased' by people they don't wish to talk to or if they are receiving any inappropriate messages they can restrict them from receiving any more and report it.

Do they understand what is personal or private information?

Making sure they are informed on what should be considered as personal or private information. Discuss with them the specific details that are okay for other people to know but also what details they should make private.

Passwords

Make sure they don't give their passwords out to friends or anyone online. Try to enforce the habit of having different passwords for different accounts to prevent accounts being broken into.

Be Aware of misinformation

Discuss with your child that not all information they see or hear online and through social media are always true. There is a lot of false information out there.
