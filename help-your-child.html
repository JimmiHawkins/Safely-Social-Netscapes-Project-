Parents:

How You Can Help Your Child While They're Online

Review their location settings

Remind your child and explain why they shouldn’t share location on posts including 'check in', chats, videos or with people they don’t know. Review their location settings on their Social media or game and talk to them about what they’re sharing online.

Be aware of the age restrictions

If you know the age restrictions of the applications your child uses or wishes to use most of them are 13, but it's better to be aware.

Are parenteral controls required

With the applications they wish to use is there specific parental controls you can implicate and make sure that you can have access to or prevent certain areas of it - For example turning of the 'personal Messaging' section.

Privacy Settings

Are their accounts private or public? Make sure to explain about the settings of privacy and what they should consider as private. Do people on a public platform need to know there location etc. Be sure to explain the dangers so they are aware and have their settings set up properly.

Do they know how to block and report other users

Being able to block and report other users is important for them to know. This will help prevent them being 'Harrased' by people they don't wish to talk to or if they are receiving any inappropriate messages they can restrict them from receiving any more and report it.

Do they understand what is personal or private information

Making sure they are informed on what should be considered as personal or private information, Discuss with them the specific details which is okay for other people to know but also what details they should make private.

Passwords

Make sure they don't give their passwords out to friends or anyone online. Try to in force the habit of having different passwords for different 'things'.

Be Aware of misinformation

Discuss with your child that not all information they see or hear online and through social media are always true. There is a lot of false information on these platforms, it may be a good idea to discuss with your child so they are aware of what they share.
