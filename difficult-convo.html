How To Have A Difficult Conversation With Your Child

Being educated on a subject before sitting down for a conversation can be extremely helpful.
Use this list of helpful hints and tips to improve how beneficial the conversation will for your child.

Stepping Stones

Don't Be Too Forceful

Don’t approach the topic too head on like a lecture, it should be more of a conversation than a confrontation.
Ask about what they think about Online Safety and their views to understand what they think, if you are too forceful this could worry them or make them feel uncomfortable, it could also make them feel like you could be unapproachable.
Additional information:
It could be a good time to talk about the topic if it was bought up at school to see what their views are on the topic then elaborate more on it into a topic to talk about online safety.

Stay balanced and Non judgemental

Be approachable. If they can trust you with being understandable and non-judgmental, they will be more inclined to tell you how they feel about a situation they might be in
Be straight to the point about some of the issues there are with Online Safety, if you are too elaborative this could confuse them on what the point of the conversation is and they may not understand the issue at hand.
Additional Information:
Sometimes, it’s good to have some examples on Online Safety from a Tv Show, movies, or online videos from people that your teen can relate to more
The CBBC website has stories and tips for staying safe online from those who have had experience.
