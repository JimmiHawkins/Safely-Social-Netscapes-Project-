What are the dangers your child faces and what you can do to prevent them

What Is Cyberbullying?

It's exactly what the name suggests. It's the act of repeated negative or hatefull messages, posts or voice messages directed to a person from one or more people.
To prevent this from happening to your child, you can make sure their account has been set-up to prevent non-friends to have the ability to send messages. Refer to the 'how to help your child page' for more information

What Is Grooming?

Engaging in inappropriate behaviour that would gradually emotionally manipulate a victim into a predatory relationship by using friendship, gifts, flattery, etc.
To help prevent this, you can first refer to the 'how to help your child' page. If this does not work then most social medias have a block feature that allows you to prevent the attacker from messaging your child.

Social Media and Mental Health.. What can you look out for?

The wellbeing of a person psychologically of your child can affected by social media content.
Make sure your child understands that social media is not real life. Check out there follow list and make sure there is not any content that promotes impossible bosy standards or other false information.

OverSharing, What is is and what it means

the act of disclosing too much information about yourself publically online. For example, having your location in your bio is not neccessary and can lead to bad consequences if the wrong person sees it.
To prevent this from happening, make sure there is no personal inforamtion posted to their profile or in their bio, username, or profile picture. For more information refer to the 'how to help your child' page.

Sexting

Sending uncensored photos, videos or text messages over the internet to other users.
To prevent this, make sure you teach your child knows the illegality of sending explicit media over the internet, and that you never know who is really behind the sceen on the other side.
